Polynomial Features Extraction

2021-07-15

Polynomial regression adds a new feature to the normal linear regression. Something very simple and requires only one change from the classic linear regression. Polynomial regression is useful if the explanatory variable and the dependent variables are related in not linear way. For example: We need food to get energy but if we eat a lot we get tired so, a parabolic pattern is formed; When we have food in a healthy amount, we get the maximum energy,but if the opposite happens we get lazy and might go to bed faster. By the way, the last sentence is absolutely, scientifically correct and is perfect representation of polynomial representation. Polynomial is still linear in that the term that we are predicting, θ\thetaθ, is linear. So polynomial regression is linear regression with polynomial features of the data. The value mmm denotes that the explanatory variable XXX and the dependent yyy are related at mthm^{th}mth polynomial degree(I cannot seem to word it better). For example: If XXX and yyy were related linearly dependent the polynomial degree(mmm) would be 1. The example about the food would be related at polynomial degree 2.

Now when we have a explanatory variable XXX having nnn entries and ppp features, the matrix can given as: X=(x11x12⋯x1px21x22⋯x2p⋮⋮⋱⋮xn1xn2⋯xnp) X = \begin{pmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12} & \cdots & x_{1p} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} & \cdots & x_{2p} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n1} & x{n2} & \cdots & x_{np} \\ \end{pmatrix} X=⎝⎛​x11​x21​⋮xn1​​x12​x22​⋮xn2​⋯⋯⋱⋯​x1p​x2p​⋮xnp​​⎠⎞​

That looks like a awfully long matrix, doesn't it? Here we first raise the power of each component of the matrix XXX from 1 to mmm and concatenate each of those to the original matrix. The shape of the matrix we are predicting changes as well: the same of the matrix θ\thetaθ becomes (m∗p)×1(m*p)\times 1(m∗p)×1. This is still a linear regression, as the component we want to predict(θ\thetaθ) is linear.

Now, lets view another way of making that same XXX matrix even longer. This one requires us to write it in a more algorithmic style.

To make this simpler, lets assume that the features ppp is even,so that we can divide the matrix XXX in two equal halves as such,

X1=(x11x21⋯x1p2x21x22⋯x2p2⋮⋮⋱⋮xn1xn2⋯xnp2) and X2=(x1(p2+1)x1(p2+2)⋯x1px2(p2+1)x2(p2+2)⋯x2p⋮⋮⋱⋮xn(p2+1)xn(p2+2)⋯xnp) X_1 = \begin{pmatrix} x_{11} & x_{21} & \cdots & x_{1\frac{p}{2}} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} & \cdots & x_{2\frac{p}{2}} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n1} & x_{n2} & \cdots & x_{n\frac{p}{2}} \end{pmatrix} \ and \ X_2 = \begin{pmatrix} x_{1(\frac{p}{2}+1)} & x_{1(\frac{p}{2}+2)} & \cdots & x_{1p} \\ x_{2(\frac{p}{2}+1)} & x_{2(\frac{p}{2}+2)} & \cdots & x_{2p} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n(\frac{p}{2}+1)} & x_{n(\frac{p}{2}+2)} & \cdots & x_{np} \end{pmatrix} X1​=⎝⎛​x11​x21​⋮xn1​​x21​x22​⋮xn2​​⋯⋯⋱⋯​x12p​​x22p​​⋮xn2p​​​⎠⎞​ and X2​=⎝⎛​x1(2p​+1)​x2(2p​+1)​⋮xn(2p​+1)​​x1(2p​+2)​x2(2p​+2)​⋮xn(2p​+2)​​⋯⋯⋱⋯​x1p​x2p​⋮xnp​​⎠⎞​ Now we perform the binomial expansion and concatenate each term of the binomial expansion without the coefficient to the original XXX.

So for a polynomial degree mmm, the additional matrix to be concatenated can be given as: Final X=(X⋯X1m⋯X1m−1X21⋯X2m)Final \ X = ( X \cdots X_1^m \cdots X_1^{m-1}X_2^1 \cdots X_2^m )Final X=(X⋯X1m​⋯X1m−1​X21​⋯X2m​) Here, X1m−1X21X_1^{m-1}X_2^1X1m−1​X21​ means element wise exponent of X1X_1X1​ to m−1m-1m−1 and X2X_2X2​ to 111, and the element wise multiplication of the result of exponential. The result is, then, concatenated to the original XXX. This is same as how polynomial feature extraction works in sk-learn.
